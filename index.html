Hi,Modified

I'm making computers design.

I'm a PhD candidate advised by Jon Cagan and Phil Leduc in Mech E at CMU. I focus on the use of automated grammar induction (DCC 2016) for identifying decision inconsistencies in traditional design and other domains. This work is currently under patent application.

As part of the Stanford Crowd Research Initiative, I am intrigued by the future of work. We've improve worker reputation signals (CSCW 2017) and introduced incentive compatibility for workers and requesters (UIST 2016). Daemo, our open source crowdsourcing platform (UIST 2015), is now live, and showcases a new approach to online open platform governance we've developed.

I mentor students at CMU and in the iSchool Inclusion Institute of Information Science (i3) at the University of Pittsburgh. We have studied how product reviews can be used to innovate at scale (iConference 2016), how research on microtask crowdsourcing can be facilitated through abstraction (forthcoming), and how novice crowd workers can conduct advanced engineering design tasks.

Recently, I've worked with Yosef Shuman to develop a definitive (and popular) answer to the question: What is service design? and with Windfire Designs to develop the Windfire Circle Tool, the best way for makers to cut circles.

I previously studied Industrial Design (MS at KAIST in Korea, BDes at RMIT in Australia, year abroad ZJU in China). I was honored to win Red Dot awards in 2009 and 2006 for my design work.

This site is under construction, so please check back soon for updates, or find out more about me by reading my résumé and by stalking me on Facebook and Twitter.
