I'm a PhD candidate in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University in the Integrated Design Innovation Group (IDIG) and the Leduc Lab.

In my doctoral work I've focused on the use of automated grammar induction (Whiting, 2016) to identify design compromises in traditional design and other applications. This work is currently under patent application.

With the Stanford Crowd Research Initiative I have worked to understand the future of work, especially from the standpoint of achieving robust open governance (Daemo, 2016), high quality work (Whiting et al. 2016) and incentive compatible interaction design (Gaikwad et al. 2016).

I also mentor students through the iSchool Inclusion Institute of Information Science (i3) at the University of Pittsburgh. With the 2014 cohort we developed a way to analyze product reviews in order to improve new products (Valderrama et al. 2016). With the 2016 cohort we are introducing a systems architecture to faster and more accurate research in microtask crowdsourcing (forthcoming publication).

I consult, run design workshops all over the world, and develop products. For example, I worked with Yosef Shuman to develop a cogent answer to the question: What is service design? and have worked with Windfire Designs to develop the Windfire Circle Tool, the best way for makers to cut circles.
