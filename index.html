Hi,

I'm making computers design.

I'm a PhD candidate in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University in the Integrated Design Innovation Group and the Leduc Lab. I focus on the use of automated grammar induction (DCC 2016) for identifying decision inconsistencies in traditional design and other domains. This work is currently under patent application.

As part of the Stanford Crowd Research Initiative, I am intrigued by the future of work. We have studied how to accurately gauge worker reputation signals (CSCW 2017) and how to make platforms with incentive compatibility for workers and requesters (UIST 2016). We have recently launched Daemo, our open source crowdsourcing platform (UIST 2015) with a new approach to online platform governance.

I mentor several students at CMU and in the iSchool Inclusion Institute of Information Science (i3) at the University of Pittsburgh. We have studied how product reviews can be used to facilitate innovation (iConference 2016), how research on microtask crowdsourcing can be made faster and more accurate with novel systems architectures (forthcoming), and how crowdsourcing can be used to conduct sophisticated engineering design tasks.

I consult, run design workshops all over the world, and develop products. For example, I worked with Yosef Shuman to develop a cogent (and popular) answer to the question: What is service design? and have worked with Windfire Designs to develop the Windfire Circle Tool, the best way for makers to cut circles.

Find out more about me by reading my Résumé or by stalking me on Facebook and Twitter.
