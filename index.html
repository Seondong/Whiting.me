Hi, Modified September 24, 2016

I'm working to enable computers to design using computational representations of intuition. Though nascent, this field stands to be an important aspect of the way we make creative decisions soon.

I'm a PhD candidate advised by Jon Cagan leads the Integrated Design Innovation Group (IDIG)Jon Cagan and Phil Leduc leads a lab focused on cellular mechanicsPhil Leduc in Mech Eng at Carnegie Mellon UniversityCMU. I focus on the use of automated grammar induction (Whiting, M. E., J. Cagan, & P.R. LeDuc, Automated Induction of General Grammars for Design. Design Computation and Cognition. 2016.DCC 2016) for identifying structural patterns in design and biological domains.

As part of the Stanford Crowd Research Initiative, I am intrigued by the future of work. We've improved worker reputation signals (CSCW 2017) and introduced incentive compatibility for workers and requesters (UIST 2016). Daemo, our open source crowdsourcing platform (UIST 2015), is now live, and showcases a new approach to online open platform governance we've developed.

I mentor students at CMU and in the iSchool Inclusion Institute of Information Science (i3) at the University of Pittsburgh. We have studied how product reviews can be used to innovate at scale (iConference 2016), how research on microtask crowdsourcing can be facilitated through abstraction (forthcoming), and how novice crowd workers can conduct advanced engineering design tasks.

Recently, I've worked with Yosef Shuman to develop a definitive (and popular) answer to the question: What is service design? and with Windfire Designs to develop the Windfire Circle Tool, the best way for makers to cut circles.

I previously studied Industrial Design (MS, KAIST in Korea; BDes, RMIT in Australia; exchange, ZJU in China). I am honored to have won Red Dot awards in 2006 and 2009 for my design work.

Though this work may seem to explore many unrelated topics, there is a central notion I feel is of utmost importance. The way we as a society and as individuals make creative decisions is one of our most human aspects. Through understanding grammar rule induction from design, learning to make individual agents in a crowd perform sophisticated judgments and creative tasks, and developing ways to better communicate about subtle design topics, I am developing a method for automating design. This site will continue to reflect my work in this area, so please check back soon for updates, or find out more by stalking me on Facebook and Twitter where I occasionally post.

Mark E. Whiting
